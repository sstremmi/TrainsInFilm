The film follows the lives of a group of performers traveling on a state-run cultural tour in China during the 1980s and 1990s. The train in the film serves as a metaphor for the changes taking place in Chinese society during this period, as well as a symbol of the characters' struggles to find meaning and purpose in their lives.

At the same time, the train journey also exposes the characters' insecurities and anxieties, as they confront the challenges of being part of a rapidly changing society.

Another important theme in the film is the search for meaning and purpose in life. The use of trains in "Platform" also reflects the importance of transportation in Chinese society. The role of trains in "Platform" is multifaceted and complex, representing both the rapid changes taking place in Chinese society and the characters' struggles to find their place in the world. The train journey becomes a metaphor for the characters' search for identity, belonging, and purpose, and it reflects the importance of transportation in Chinese society.
